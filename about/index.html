About

2 min

Hi Im Ekky, a Senior Site Reliability Engineer.

I’m a lifelong learner, a systems thinker, and a builder at heart.

My journey in tech has always been driven by curiosity. I don’t just work with systems, I obsess over how they behave, how they break, and how to make them better, faster, and more resilient. Whether it’s infrastructure, automation, security, observability, or developer tooling, I find joy in diving deep into complexity and emerging with clarity, structure, and solutions that make a real impact.

What defines me most is my love of learning. I’m the kind of person who reads RFCs for fun, browses Medium and Wikipedia late at night, and gets excited by a good problem, technical or philosophical. I thrive when I’m learning something new or helping someone else solve a thorny problem. I believe that every system, whether technical, organizational, or human, can be understood, improved, and scaled with the right mindset and approach.

I call myself a philomath, someone who finds joy in learning anything. Outside of tech, I’m deeply interested in philosophy, history, psychology, nature, and human systems. I believe that well-rounded engineers aren’t just great coders or architects, they’re communicators, thinkers, and collaborators who can bridge disciplines and work with diverse people toward common goals.

I’m also passionate about:

  • Building tools that empower others and remove friction from development workflows
  • Making infrastructure and operations more accessible, scalable, and reliable through code
  • Sharing knowledge and mentoring others in the community or within teams
  • Designing systems that don’t just “work,” but evolve well under pressure and change
  • Balancing technical rigour with empathy for real-world use cases and developer experience

If there’s one thing I aim for in my career, it’s to be the kind of engineer that teammates can rely on, not just to write solid code or manage infrastructure, but to ask the right questions, drive clarity, and leave systems (and teams) better than I found them.

Outside of work, you’ll find me exploring new topics, watching deep-dive YouTube content, hanging out with friends,working on DIY side projects, or just going out and explore the world.
